## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### Voice & Tone
- Write in **British English** (optimise, customise, colour, recognise, licence)
- Confident but not arrogant — the tone of a trusted expert, not a salesperson
- Address the reader directly as "you"
- Use "I" when writing in Nathaniel's first-person voice (About page, personal posts)
- Use "we" only when the context clearly suits a studio/agency framing

### Structure
- H1: One per post, contains primary keyword
- Short paragraphs: **2-3 sentences maximum** in blog posts
- Bold the key takeaway sentence in each section
- Subheadings every 300–400 words
- Always include a FAQ section in blog posts
- Always end with a CTA linking to a relevant service page or contact page

### SEO
- Primary keyword density: 1–1.5%
- Keyword in H1, first 100 words, 2–3 H2s, meta title, meta description
- 3–5 internal links per article using descriptive anchor text
- 2–3 external links to authoritative sources (WordPress.org, Google, industry stats)
- Meta title: 50–60 characters, include "| Designify" where space allows
- Meta description: 150–160 characters, include keyword + soft CTA

### What to Avoid

#### Banned Words — NEVER use these under any circumstances:
**Filler/hedging words**:
- actually, usually, normally, generally
- basically, simply, obviously, clearly
- very, really, quite, rather, fairly
- just, even, still, already

**Weak qualifiers**:
- somewhat, relatively, arguably
- in some ways, to some extent
- kind of, sort of

**AI-sounding phrases**:
- "In today's digital landscape…" or any variation
- "It's worth noting that…"
- "It is important to note that…"
- "As mentioned above/below"
- "Delve into" / "dive into"
- "In order to" (use "to" instead)
- "Due to the fact that" (use "because" instead)
- "At the end of the day"
- "Game-changer" / "game changing"
- "Leverage" (when used as a verb)
- "Utilize" (use "use" instead)
- "Ensure" (use "make sure" instead)
- "Provide" (be specific about what is being given)
- "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally" (find more natural transitions)
- "In conclusion" / "To summarise"

**Overclaiming**:
- "World-class", "best-in-class", "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art"
- "Revolutionary", "groundbreaking", "unprecedented"
- "Our team" or "team of experts" — Nathaniel works solo

**Formatting**:
- Emojis in body text (only acceptable in CTAs/conclusions, sparingly)
- Passive voice — always rewrite to active voice
